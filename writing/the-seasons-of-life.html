The Seasons of Life

most people float by and let life happen to them. same routines, same trivial questions, same days on repeat. slowly, everything starts blending together. one week becomes one month. one month becomes one year. then suddenly, you look back and wonder where all the time went.

the solution? be more intentional.

"spend more time making the big decisions. there are basically three really big decisions you make in your early life: where you live, who you're with, and what you do."

— naval ravikant

for me, one of the best ways to avoid floating is to think in four-month arcs.

maybe it is because that is the rhythm i have always known. a school term was four months. an internship was four months. summer break was four months. naturally, i started seeing each block as its own dedicated chapter.

four months is long enough to make meaningful strides, but short enough to stay focused and avoid getting lost in uncertainty. you can build a skill, work on a business, get in shape, or completely reinvent yourself. it gives your life structure without forcing you to planning too far ahead since things can and will change so you'll need to adapt and pivot.

when i was younger, i thought productivity meant being constantly "on". i thought the goal was to grind forever and maybe take that two weeks vacation to relax. but the more i have stepped into the world of creative work and building things, the more i realize that is not an optimal way to live.

a healthier approach is to move in sprints. an athletes operating system is to go through intense periods of training, pushing their limits before an equally "intense" period of recovery. it's part of the game. so dedicate a season to lock in and go all in on that, but then step back to reset and reflect. let yourself breathe before stepping on the gas again.

life already works this way when you think about it. nature has seasons. stock markets have cycles. people are no different.

some seasons feel like everything is clicking. you are stacking wins, moving with momentum, and you feel unstoppable. but other seasons feel heavier. you are uncertain, heartbroken, struggling, lost, or just not where you want to be.
