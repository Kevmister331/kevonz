My investment philosophy is straightforward:

identify asymmetric opportunities at the frontier of innovation.

I spend more time thinking about where the world is headed than where it is today. Once I develop conviction in a long-term trend, I look for the companies best positioned to benefit from it. Fundamental metrics like financial statements validate the thesis, but they rarely originate it.

In high school I wanted to build a PC but I couldn't justify the cost of a GPU, so I bought NVIDIA stock instead. That experience reinforced an idea I still believe today: if you have conviction in where the world is headed, owning the businesses driving that change can be more valuable than simply consuming the products themselves. It was the same reasoning behind my early conviction in Tesla and the broader transition toward electric vehicles.
