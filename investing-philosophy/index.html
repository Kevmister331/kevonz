My investment philosophy is simple:

Find asymmetric opportunities at the frontier of innovation.

I think deeply about where the world is headed and which trends are likely to shape it. Once I develop conviction, I look for the companies best positioned to benefit. Financials validate the thesis; but they rarely create it.

In high school, I wanted to build a PC but could not justify the cost of a GPU, so I bought NVIDIA stock instead. I saw early that demand for computing power was growing faster than supply. The same instinct led me to Tesla and the broader shift toward electric vehicles. In both cases, I was rewarded for recognizing where demand was moving before it became obvious.
